Introduction

Understanding student academic performance is essential for improving educational outcomes and promoting long-term success. One of the most widely used indicators of academic achievement is Grade Point Average (GPA), a numerical representation of a student's academic standing. GPA not only influences college admissions and scholarship eligibility, but it also reflects a student’s study habits, support systems, and access to educational resources. Monitoring and analyzing GPA can provide valuable insights into how various factors such as parental involvement, study behaviors, and participation in extracurricular activity contribute to student achievement. A low GPA may signal challenges such as lack of support or engagement, while a high GPA often correlates with strong academic habits and a supportive environment.

Beyond individual outcomes, GPA trends can also reveal broader patterns within the education system, including disparities linked to socioeconomic status or access to academic support. These patterns can help educators and policymakers develop more effective strategies to address gaps in performance and ensure all students have equal opportunity in the classroom.

To learn more about academic performance trends and education research, visit the National Center for Education Statistics or explore reports from the Education Week Research Center.

Overall we chose to have our project focus on exploring the dynamics behind student GPA using a variety of visual tools. By examining the connections between academic performance and different personal, familial, and behavioral factors, we aim to better understand what drives success in school and how it can be supported across diverse student populations.

Introduction to the Data

Our analysis makes use of a single comprehensive dataset titled “students-performance.csv”, which contains detailed information on 2,392 high school students. The dataset includes variables related to students’ academic performance, personal demographics, study habits, parental involvement, and participation in extracurricular activities. These features allow for a well-rounded exploration of the various factors that may influence a student’s GPA. The dataset contains 2,392 rows and 15 features. A summary of the variables is provided in the table below:

Variable Name Details
Demographics
StudentID Unique identifier for each student (from 1001 to 3392)
Age Age of student (ranging from 15 to 18)
Gender Gender of student (0=Male, 1=Female)
Ethnicity Coded ethnicity (0=Caucasian, 1=African American, 2=Asian, 3=Other)
Parental Factors
ParentalEducation Parental education level (0=None, 1=High School, 2=Some College, 3=Bachelor’s, 4=Higher)
ParentalSupport Level of parental support (0=None to 4=Very High)
Study & Academic Factors
StudyTimeWeekly Number of hours spent studying weekly (0 to 20)
Tutoring Tutoring status (0=No, 1=Yes)
Absences Number of school absences in the academic year (0 to 30)
GPA Students GPA on a 2.0 to 4.0 scale
GradeClass Categorized GPA: 0=A, 1=B, 2=C, 3=D, 4=F
Extracurricular Involvement
Extracurricular Participation in extracurriculars (0= No, 1=Yes)
Sports Participation in sports (0= No, 1=Yes)
Music Participation in music activities (0= No, 1=Yes)
Volunteering Participation in volunteering activities (0= No, 1=Yes)
